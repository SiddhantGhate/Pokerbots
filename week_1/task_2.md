2A:
There are 3 cases:

1. More than k people contribute- there will be no nash equilibrium as if other people's actions are fixed, the given player will always prefer to not contribute and still get the benefits

2. Less than k people contribute- Again there is no nash equilibrium as if other people's actions are fixed, the given player will choose to not contribute as anyways there is no benefit of contributing as they wont receive any benefits.
However, if all the people dont contribute, then we reach a nash equilibrium, as now no one will choose to contribute( nobody will want to change their current action).

3. k people contribute- This is a nash equilibrium as the people who contributed will continue to contribute or else they will lose the benefits and the people who are not contributing will continue to not contribute as they are anyways getting the benefits

2B.1:
1. Exactly or more than m hunters hunt the stag- not a nash equilibrium as the hunters who are hunting the hare will now want to hunt the stag. However, the case when all hunters hunt the stag is a nash equilibrium.

2. Less than m hunters hunt the stag- not a nash equilibrium as the hunters hunting the stag will want to hunt the hare. However, the case when all hunters are hunting the hare is a nash equilibrium.
